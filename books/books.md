C Karl von Frisch classic (the guy who first described and analysed the waggle dance). "Aus dem Leben der Bienen" (think the english equivalent should be Bees: their vision chemical senses and languange)

"Phänomen Hongbiene (the buzz about bees)" by Jürgen Tautz. However both of theses books are just on honey bees, its hard to find sth about all bees/there are simply too many .

I love C. Micheners "Bees of the World" very much because it includes almost all bee Taxa, but it is mainly a (very good) identification key and has always just a brief description of the biology of the Taxa (and a brief general introduction at the begining of the book). There are two really nice books on bumble bees existent, also one classic "The humble bee" be Sladen and the more recent "Behaviour Ecology of the Bumblebee" by Goulson. Another nice book on the asian honeybee races and a bit of evolutionary theory on bees would be "Honey bees of Borneo" by Gudrun and Nikolaus Königer (a really cute couple who does very good bee reasearch together since ages...they still climb on trees to search for bees etc etc). A nice general book on beekeeping would be "The world history on beekeeping and honey hunting" by Eva Crane...Guess that's more than enough at first
